The Root and STEM Colleges

The ideal of the Unity-Versity School root and STEM Colleges structure is that in order to effectively tackle the difficult disciplines of study in STEM requires addressing the root barriers of learning for BIPOC groups including educational, social, legal, and economic injustice, fragile families and amplified traumatic experiences. Unity-Versity School is about bring together or unifying all people groups (intentionally seeking BIPOC), the STEM disciplines, diverse families and healing wholeness to traumatized individuals.

DEI College

In the U.S. our history of slavery, racism and discrimination has never been reconciled. Today, African Americans live daily with the on-going impact of systemic racism. Diversity, Equity and Inclusion efforts help raise the level of awareness, allying and action to rectify these injustices. These efforts can help build a bridge to compassionate curiousity, empathetic understanding, unconditional acceptance, and humble service to justice.

Healthy Families College

In my dissertation I found positively statistically significant associations between the quantity and quality of the biological father involvement in the non-intact families and young adult child outcomes. The results seemed to indicate the importance of the role of the father; even in a non-intact family where the father spends time apart from his children, he is still able to influence positive outcomes of his children. Another important finding is the strong correlation between a supportive family as a child and positive outcomes as an adult.

Trauma Resilency College

In late 1990s, the Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACE) research supported two important findings, the first was how common trauma is for all of every person, the second was trauma’s connection with negative health outcomes. Of the over 17,000 adults who participated in the study 67% had experienced one or more traumatic experiences as a child. And the traumatic childhood experiences were exponentially linked to poor and potentially deadly health outcomes as adults (Felitti et al., 1998).

Trauma Resilency College

My position on Trauma Resilency is that trauma results from an event, series of events or set of circumstances that is experienced by an individual as physically or emotionally harmful or life threatening and that has lasting adverse effects on the individual’s functioning and mental, physical, social, emotional or spiritual well-being. Lack of trauma awareness, building resilency and recovery hampers individuals from healthy individual and family functioning.
