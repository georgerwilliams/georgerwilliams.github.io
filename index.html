George R. Williams, Ph.D.

How can I serve you? My life mission is reflected in the words of Nobel Peace Prize winner Albert Schweitzer, “The purpose of human life is to serve, and to show compassion and the will to help others.” For almost two decades my life work has been driven by the passion to bring innovative, relevant, engaging, and competency enriching services and resources to helping professionals and organizations who serve families and communities. These services include keynotes, workshops, training, programming, curriculum development, writing, research and consulting.

Let's work together! My education as a marriage and family therapist and family studies researcher and my experiences working corporate, not-for-profit, faith community, consultant and state government leadership positions and my skills as a manager, trainer, presenter, curriculum developer, program implementer and grant writer give me a unique edge to achieving your vision to strengthen youth, fathers, families in your community. It is my relentless pursuit to create superior experiences that lead to an uplifting community impact.

Experience.

The Father your Child Needs

Executive director at the National Center for Fathering for over a decade, working with talented and passionate staff to inspire and equip fathers at the local, state and national level. My highest honor and inspiration was working with thousands of men who are striving to be the fathers their children need.

Helping America's Youth

Executive director at Avery's Village, a youth residential center for foster boys ages 10-18. Assistant director for the state of Kansas involved in advocating for foster children and creating programming for preschool children. A youth pastor for an urban churches in mid and south Kansas City and former president for Urban Youth Leadership.

Train | Consult | Program

Served clients that range from Health Care Foundation of Greater Kansas City, Mid-America Regional Council Head Start Program, the Administration for Children and Families to the Kauffman Scholars, Inc. Projects include fathering projects, trauma-informed care, cultural proficeny enrichment and implicit bias.

Stand Up for Black Lives

Advocate for seeing the plight of African Americans, listening to their stories of injustice and pain and working to do all I can to help. A member of the Johnson County NAACP. As a consultant created the Cultural Proficiency Enrichment training and Implicit Bias training conduct for the Kansas City Chamber of Commerce.

Mission | Family | Interest

This mission statement gives insight into who I am called to be. My family is God's gift to me and my greatest treasure on earth. My gratitude for my family is a motivation for why I do what I do. I grew up with my father, mother and five siblings in the racially and ethnically diverse city in a poor household by federal guidelines.
