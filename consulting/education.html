Education

My self-assessment as a student is characterized as a life long lover of learning for the purpose of application. In other words, not knowledge just for the sake of knowing but for the sake of doing.

My 1981 post high school educational journey began in Kansas at Cloud County Community College where I attended on a track scholarship. The next year I transferred to Kansas State University and majored in Computer Science (CS). My brother was an electrical engineer and he stirred my interest in exploring the promising field of computer programming.

During my senior year at KSU my interest was aroused in the social helping professions and the idea that I might later return to school for that training. I graduated with a B.S. in CS 1984. After spending thirteen years in the computer science industry in Oklahoma and California, I felt a calling to serve others. In 1997 I began attending California State University Dominguez Hills to fulfill the requirements of entering a master of science in clinical psychology.

In 1998 I took a position at a fathering non-profit and the next year I began the pursuit of a masters in marriage and family therapy(MFT) to provide theory to guide me in the practice of helping fathers, mothers and children in family relationships. I graduated with my M.S. in MFT in 2001. In 2007 I began pursuing a doctorate in family studies(FS) as a way to complete the loop of theory, research and practice. I needed to be able to research the theories and promising practices to help the fathers, mothers and children in family relationships. I will graduate with my Ph.D. in FS in 2015.
