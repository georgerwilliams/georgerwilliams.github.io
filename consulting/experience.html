Employment

Nation-wide: National Center for Fathering
Working as executive director at the National Center for Fathering was a revolutionary experience for me. The annual operating budget for the center was over $2 million. For 10 years I worked with other talented and passionate staff to inspire and equip fathers at the local, state and national level to be the fathers their children needed. I successfully experienced every aspect of helping to run a not-for-profit organization.

Over the years I have been enriched by the opportunities to have spoken at state and national conferences, written for and appeared in national magazines, trained trainers across the country, appeared on national news, been a reoccurring guest on a talk show, held workshops for a professional football team, testified as an expert witness before a Senate subcommittee, authored curricula, became a contributing author for an anthology on fatherhood, and visited the first Lady’s White House conference and reception. But my highest honor and inspiration was working with thousands of men who are striving to be the fathers their children need.

State-Wide: State of Kansas
For the past 5 years I've been priviledge to provide support to Kansas citizens in my roles as assistant director and special assistant in the Department for Children and Families (DCF). Served on the DCF Secretary’s executive team, meeting twice a week for two years for agency reporting, planning and team decisions. Wrote requests for proposal, reviewed, and awarded grants and contracts for millions of dollars of social programming. Led, planned, developed, and implemented the innovative Kansas Strong Dads program with a $1.5 million budget with 19 partnering organizations, in which hundreds of Early Head Start families were assisted. Created and delivered trainings for hundreds of Kansas citizens and agency staff for fatherhood, leadership and mentoring. Currently working on social worker retention by developing and delivering CEU courses for DCF's 1,500 social workers.

As a former director of Addiction and Prevention Services I was the designated single state authority for the field. Directed a staff of 23 including five managers with an operating budget of $46 million. Experiences include hiring staff, performance reviews, progress reporting, team building, regular management team meetings, site visits, program policies, automated systems and budget meetings. Worked with over 200 providers of substance abuse disorder (SUD) and various associations, stakeholders and community partners on substance use disorder policy, prevention, rehabilitation and recovery of clients.

Consulting

National Center for Fathering (NCF), Shawnee, KS Director of the Homeless Fathering Project (one year grant). Secured a $50,000 grant by the Health Care Foundation of Greater KC for the planning and oversight of a program to work with men in one homeless shelter and two residential treatment centers. Led them in creating and maintaining the social support with family to facilitate long term rehabilitation and self-sufficiency that reduces the likelihood of family violence.

Mid-America Head Start (MAHS), Kansas City, MO Lead Consultant for Fathering Initiative (one year contract). Maintained oversight and analyzed expenditures of a $35,000 contract to work within the policy, standards and guidelines of four delegate Head Starts that served 3,000 families. Provided the planning and oversight of an effort to assist their staff and fathers with assessment, identification of strategies, training and programs for the purpose of the planning and implementation of delegate father engagement plans.

The Kansas City Community Center (KCCC), Kansas City, MO Director of the Father Violence Prevention Project (one year grant). Secured a $90,000 grant by the Health Care Foundation of Greater KC and worked with residential re-entry clients in three facilities from state and federal prisons and outpatient clients in various diversion programs. This transitional rehabilitation program prepared the men for re-entry into families by assisting in reducing the risks of family violence. A third party evaluation confirmed the effectiveness of program. Also awarded $25,000 federal fatherhood capacity building grant which included organization leadership training.

Kauffman Scholars, Inc., Kansas City, MO Consultant for Males to Men Program (one year contract) A $45,000 contract to design and implement the Males to Men program to assist in the transition of low-income inner city male students and their fathers using my Males to Men curriculum. The purpose of this program was rehabilitation of the youth socially and educationally in the student’s attitudes, values and skills related to retention and excellence in educational and personal success. The program evaluation confirmed the effectiveness of the program.

Education

I am a lifelong learning, not for the sake of knowledge but for the sake of application. My post high school educational journey began at a Community College where I attended on a track scholarship. The next year I transferred to Kansas State University and majored in Computer Science (CS). I graduated with a bachelor of science in 1984.

In 1999 I began the pursuit of a masters in marriage and family therapy (MFT) at Friends University to provide theory to guide me in the practice of helping fathers, mothers and children in family relationships. I graduated with my masters in 2001.

In 2007 I began pursuing a doctorate in family studies(FS) to complete the loop of theory, research and practice. I needed to be able to research the theories and promising practices to help the fathers, mothers and children in family relationships. I graduated with my doctorate in 2016.
