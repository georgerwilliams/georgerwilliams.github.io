Mission Statement

This mission statement gives insight into who I am called to be. I know it rhymes but it made it easier to memorize.

I am a spiritual child of Father God above, pursuing Him each moment in worshipful love.

I am a man striving for integrity true, respect, responsibility and righteousness my internal glue

I am the husband of Trudy my wife, loving, serving faithful for the rest of my life.

I am the father of one daughter, three sons, caring and sharing daily with each one.

I am a servant in service to others, building and rebuilding my sisters and brothers.

My Family

My family is God's gift to me and my greatest treasure on earth. Starting with my darling wife of 31 years, Trudy. Each of our children are unique and valued. They are all "kind, smart and important" and they are distinctly gifted. My gratitude for my family is a motivation for why I do what I do.

Biographical

George R. Williams, Ph.D. provides leadership and assistance with the fatherhood initiatives for the state of Kansas as a assistant director in the Department for Children and Families. George has been actively involved over the past 13 years as the former executive director of the Urban Father Child Partnership in developing and delivering fathering education and programming in various settings throughout communities across the country. His presentations feature thoughtful insights and an engaging, passionate style. George also writes and performs powerful dramatizations, poetry and songs that illustrate the challenges of fathers.

George is a nationally recognized fathering and family expert that has spoken and taught at national conferences including the National PTA Conference, the National Welfare Reform Conference, National Fatherhood Summit, Annual Fathers Behind Bars Conference, the National Association of Basketball Coaches Conference, Family Support America and the Head Start Institute on Father Involvement Conference. He served on the expert panel of the Strengthening Families with Children Born Out-Of-Wedlock research project con-ducted by Princeton University and sponsored by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. He has testified as an expert fathering witness before a Senate Sub-committee and was invited to join the National Urban League’s Commission of African American Males.

George is a contributing author to the book, Why Fathers Count and the textbook, Fundamentals of Early Childhood Education 4th Edition. He has authored three fathering curricula: Quenching the Father Thirst, the Kansas Strong Dads Handbook, and R.E.A.D. (Reconnecting Education and Dads) to Kids. His has appeared on national television as a spokesperson for urban fathering on CNN, FOX News, PBS, ION TV and TBN. He has appeared in print as a “Phenomenal Father” by Ebony Magazine and in Black Enterprise and JET Magazine.

George earned his Bachelor of Science degree in computer science from Kansas State University (Manhattan, KS.) He earned his Master of Science degree from Friends University (Wichita, KS) in marriage and family therapy and is a lifetime member in Psi Chi, the National Honor Society in Psychology. He is a graduate from the DeVos Urban Leadership Initiative’s ministry training program, a past president of Urban Youth Leadership. He has a Doctorate of Philosophy in family studies, College of Human Ecologoy, School of Family Studies and Human Services at Kansas State University. He serves in leadership at Cross Point Community Church of the Nazarene. George and Trudy, his wife of thirty-one years, have three boys and a daughter and live in the greater Kansas City area.
