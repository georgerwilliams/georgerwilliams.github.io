Helping America's Youth

The Foster Care Crisis

Families in America are facing unprecedent challenges with the combination of the national opiod epidemic and the current COVID-19 pandemic and the related fallout. Over the past decade we have seen a increase in the number of foster care caseloads nation wide to the latest reported number for FY 2019 of over 423,000. These numbers bring the greatest challenge to child welfare officials in many states for children who have been removed from the families to find appropriate placements.

Children are placed in a variety of settings based on the child's needs, including placement with relatives, unrelated foster parents, group homes or residential treatment centers. States are reducing the number of children served in congregate care. These states agree that children are best served in family settings with relatives or foster families while they work to get their families healthy for reunification.

Part of the solution is for compassionate families willing to open their homes for these children in need. And even though not everyone can foster children, everyone can do something. We can look for and find ways to raise the level of awareness of the need for foster families. Or we can seek out foster families in our sphere of influence and be supportive to the foster families and/or children.

CINC Child In Need of Care

The foster care crisis has hit the state of Kansas with about 7,000 children in the child welfare system and less than half that number of foster homes. CINC is a poem about the plight of the Kansas foster children and the need for foster families.
